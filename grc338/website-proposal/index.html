About

Izakaya Yamato Ramen House is a small, independently family owned ramen restaurant passed down fourth generation that first opened its doors in 1932. Located in a small town called "The City of Guadalupe". The restaurant specializes in old traditional Japanese ramen that is prepared with home-made broths, daily hand-made fresh noodles, and obtain freshly sourced ingredients by our local farmers. The idea focuses on serving authentic Japanese flavors in a relaxing, warm environment whether you need to get a quick meal or relaxing in our warmth dining experiences.

Fun Fact: Izakaya Yamato (居酒屋大和) is translated as a Japanese-style pub or tavern. The word Izakaya (居酒屋) means to "stay" or "drink" and "place". Yamoto (大和) means "Great Harmoney".

From the warmth of traditional home-made broths to our freshly made noodles bring you a authentic Japanese flavors, Izakaya Yamato Ramen House is designed to evoke Japanese sense of retreat inviting, grounded to touched with the feeling you being in Japan.
